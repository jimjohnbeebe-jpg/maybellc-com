# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Prospective clients, partners, and customers researching Maybe LLC or one of
its three ventures (RepairVector, Zephyrphoto, RescueRich) — typically
arriving via search, a direct link, or word of mouth, on desktop or mobile.
They're evaluating credibility fast: is this a real, competent operator? The
job to be done is quick orientation (what does Maybe LLC do, what does it
run) followed by a confident click-through — to a venture's own site, or to
`mailto:info@maybellc.com`. Some are veterans or veteran-adjacent readers for
whom "proudly veteran owned" is a meaningful trust signal, not decoration.

## Product Purpose

A single-page company site that establishes Maybe LLC as a disciplined,
technically credible operator and routes visitors to its three live
ventures. It exists to convert cold traffic into a click to a venture site
or a direct email — nothing more elaborate. Success is a visitor who
understands the operating thesis (rational development + modern technology,
applied to real problems) in one scroll, and trusts it enough to follow
through.

## Brand Personality

Disciplined, rational, understated. Evidence over hype — the copy already
says as much ("decisions made on evidence, not hype," "ship what holds up
under real use"). The existing dark "garage" theme (diagnostic-shop dark
background, torque-blue accent, signal/fault/clear traffic-light status
accents, monospace for URLs and technical labels) is the correct register:
technical precision, not startup flash. Confidence is conveyed through
restraint and craft, not scale of claims.

## Anti-references

Generic SaaS/AI-slop defaults this skill already bans by default: gradient
hero text, the hero-metric template, cream/sand/beige "warm neutral" body
backgrounds, tiny uppercase tracked eyebrows stacked on every section,
numbered 01/02/03 scaffolding used reflexively rather than because a real
sequence exists, identical icon-heading-text card grids, glassmorphism as
decoration. Also avoid flashy startup hype — bold unearned gradients, bouncy
elastic motion, oversized unsubstantiated claims. The existing numbered
venture/pillar indices (01/02/03) and mono-font URL chrome are a deliberate,
earned exception tied to the diagnostic/garage motif — not the AI-eyebrow
reflex — and should stay that way rather than multiply into decoration.

## Design Principles

- **Practice what you preach.** The site is built exactly as its own copy
  describes Maybe LLC's work: rational, disciplined, no unnecessary
  complexity. No framework, no build step, no ornament without a reason.
- **Evidence over hype.** No unsubstantiated superlatives, no decorative
  gradients or motion that isn't communicating something real.
- **The garage motif is earned, not decorative.** Diagnostic-shop dark
  theme, traffic-light status accents, monospace technical labels — these
  exist because RepairVector's actual product domain is automotive
  diagnostics. Extend this motif deliberately; don't dilute it with generic
  SaaS patterns, and don't let it curdle into cosplay.
- **Credibility through restraint.** Trust signals (veteran-owned badges,
  "documentation verified" language, real venture screenshots) carry more
  weight than persuasive copy. Let them stand plainly.
- **One page, no filler.** Every section earns its place by routing the
  visitor toward a venture link or contact — no padding sections just to
  look complete.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA baseline: ≥4.5:1 body text contrast, ≥3:1 large text, visible
`:focus-visible` states, keyboard navigability, `alt` text on every image
(already largely in place per `.claude/rules/architecture.md` and
`.claude/rules/conventions.md`). No AAA target and no colorblind-specific
remediation requested for the signal/fault/clear status dots at this time —
flagged as a known consideration if the color palette changes, but not an
open requirement.
